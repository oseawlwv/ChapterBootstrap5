Article 1 - Dues

1.1

All members shall pay local dues in addition to the state dues.

1.2

Local dues shall be determined by a 2⁄3 vote of the active members present at a chapter meeting.

1.3

Current per capita dues are one dollar and fifty cents ($1.50) per month worked.

Article 2 - Officers

2.1 Eligibility

In order to be eligible to run for executive office, candidates shall be active and shall have attended at least two meetings within the current school year.

2.2 Officers and responsibilities

2.2.1 President - The president serves as the leader and advisor to the chapter and is the official spokesperson of the chapter. The president serves as chair of the executive committee, presides over all Chapter meetings, and, by virtue of the office, serves on all committees with the exception of the elections committee. The president also assumes any other such duties as are required.

  • 2.2.1.1 The Sick Leave Bank Committee:
    The President and one other member, appointed by the President, shall serve on the combined district Sick Leave Bank Committee.
  • 2.2.1.2 The Reclassification Committee:
    The President and one other member, appointed by the President, shall serve on the combined district Reclassification Committee.

2.2.2 Vice-President - The vice-president serves on the executive committee and assumes all such duties as required by the president. The vice-president assumes the duties of the president in his or her absence or when called upon by the chair to do so and assumes any other such duties as are required. The vice-president serves as chair of the worksite organizers committee.

2.2.3 Secretary - The secretary serves on the executive committee and accurately records the minutes of all chapter meetings and executive committee meetings. The secretary assists the president in communication as needed and holds all digital records in safekeeping. The secretary shall keep all committee membership lists up-to-date and be able to provide them upon request. This listing shall be made available to all members.

2.2.4 Treasurer - The treasurer serves on the executive committee and prepares and maintains an accurate digital record of each member of the chapter including name , membership status, date admitted to the chapter and dues payments. The treasurer receives and distributes all monies of the chapter, follows standard bookkeeping practices, and keeps an accurate digital record in safekeeping. The treasurer serves as chair of the finance committee and works with them to create a proposed budget that will be voted on at the first chapter meeting of the new school year, prepares regular financial reports to be distributed at each chapter meeting and prepares an end-of-the-year financial statement to be submitted at the final chapter meeting of the year.

2.2.5 Officers shall turn all correspondence, records, materials, electronic 15 data and equipment of the office over to their successors or to a 16 designated union representative.

2.3 The Executive Committee shal:

2.3.1 Recognize its responsibility to ensure adherence to West Linn-Wilsonville School District’s accepted legal standards and ethical norms.

2.3.2 Not violate any provision of the chapter constitution and bylaws.

2.3.3 Not misappropriate, embezzle or improperly or illegally use chapter funds.

2.3.4 Make decisions about Chapter operations and the use or disposition of assets solely in terms of the benefits to the chapter and its general membership and shall not be influenced by any profit or other personal benefit to the individuals affiliated with the Chapter who take part in the decision.

2.3.5 Hold ethics, integrity, fairness and concern for the Chapter’s welfare as the guiding standard for their actions.

2.3.6 Make every effort to remain on good working terms with administrators, district office personnel and school board members.

2.3.7 Be accountable and transparent in and regarding every action.

2.3.8 Have the right to convene committees as needed.

2.4 Term Limits

2.4.1 Officers serve one-year terms and shall be limited to three consecutive terms in any one position. Individuals who have reached the three-term limit may then choose to run for a different office.

2.4.2After vacating office, the past president shall not serve on the executive committee for a period of one year. The past president may be called upon to act in a non-voting advisory capacity during that year. After this one-year time period the past president may run for executive office again.

2.4.3 Officer terms begin the moment the officers are installed.

Time spent filling a vacancy will not be counted toward an officer’s term limit.

2.5 Recall of Officers

2.5.1 Any officer may be recalled from office with a vote of no confidence for conduct tending to injure the good name of the organization, disturb its well-being, or hamper it in its work. Specific conduct includes, but is not limited to, the reasons for discipline or dismissal listed in the Classified Bargaining Agreement.

2.5.2 A recall may be initiated by a petition of thirty percent (30%) of the active members or by 3⁄4 of the Executive Committee. The petition shall state the specific reasons for the recall and shall be presented to the Executive Committee and to the officer subject to the recall action.

2.5.3 Upon receipt of the petition, the Executive Committee shall schedule a special meeting to be held not less than fifteen (15) days nor more than thirty (30) days following its receipt. Notice of this meeting shall be sent to the membership no less than ten days in advance of the meeting and shall include the date, time and location of this meeting.

2.5.4 At this meeting the officer in question shall be afforded the opportunity to speak in his or her defense and the vote shall be conducted.

2.5.5 The vote shall be by secret ballot. A 3⁄4 majority of the members who participated in the vote is required in order to recall the officer in question.

2.6 Vacancies

2.6.1 Resignations should be submitted to the Executive Committee in writing.

2.6.2 A vacancy of the office of president shall be filled by the vice-president.

2.6.3 For vacancies in the offices of Vice-President, Secretary or Treasurer, the executive committee shall collect nominations for the vacant roll and schedule a special meeting where an election shall be held.

2.6.4 The election shall be by secret ballot and shall be conducted by the Elections Committee.

2.6.5 The Executive Committee shall notify the membership at least fifteen days in advance of the meeting. Such notice shall include the nominees, as well as the date, time and location of this meeting as well as the method of voting and the voting window.

2.6.6 The voting window shall be approved by the Executive Committee and announced at the special meeting.

ARTICLE III - Committees

3.1

All committees are appointed by and report to the Executive Committee except where stated otherwise.

3.2

Standing Committees are permanent and ongoing committees responsible for performing necessary functions of the Chapter in particular areas. Their names and duties are as follows:

3.2.1 The Worksite Organizer Committee shall:

  • (3.2.1.1) Be appointed by the Executive Committee with the advice and consent of the employees represented and have at least one but not more than three members from each site represented.
  • (3.2.1.2) Distribute OSEA information and membership packets to new employees and provide multiple opportunities for employees to join OSEA.
  • (3.2.1.3) Distribute written Chapter communication.
  • (3.2.1.4) Inform the Executive Committee of classified employee problems and concerns.
  • (3.2.1.5) Attend Chapter meetings.
  • (3.2.1.6) Maintain the Chapter OSEA information boards at each worksite.
  • (3.2.1.7) Coordinate with the Executive Committee and with designated committee members when necessary to gather information, facilitate committee action, assist with elections, or whatever else is necessary for the common good.
  • (3.2.1.8) Ask for additional support from the employees at their site when necessary in order to accomplish any of the above tasks.

3.2.2 The Finance/Budget Committee shall:

  • (3.2.2.1) Have no more than five members.
  • (3.2.2.2) Ensure audits are performed annually – an internal audit performed on even years by a specially-appointed auditor and an external audit performed on odd years by the OSEA State Office.
  • (3.2.2.3) Prepare a proposed budget to be ready at the start of June for approval by the chapter at the next chapter meeting.
  • (3.2.2.4) Assist the Treasurer as requested in performing the duties of the office in compliance with the practices and procedures 6 described in the bylaws.

3.2.3 The Elections Committee shall:

  • (3.2.3.1) Serve from January to December of the same calendar year.
  • (3.2.3.2) Have no more than five members.
  • (3.2.3.3) Conduct all elections in compliance with the practices and 11 procedures described in the bylaws.

3.2.4 The Website Committee shall:

  • (3.2.4.1) Have no more than five members.
  • (3.2.4.2) Maintain the Chapter’s web presence and content at the direction of the Executive Committee.
  • (3.2.4.3) Assure that all current and future members of the website committee can manage website content.

3.3 Special Committes

Special Committees are appointed for a short time and for a specific event or purpose and disbanded immediately afterward. Their names and duties include, but are not limited to:

3.3.1 The Negotiations Committee shall:

  • (3.3.1.1) Be chaired by the President.
  • (3.3.1.2) Include the Executive Committee members.
  • (3.3.1.3) Develop a negotiations package based on information obtained from the membership and with assistance from the OSEA Field Representative.
  • (3.3.1.4) Have no more than two representatives from the following classifications whenever possible:
    • Bookkeeper, Secretary
    • Paraeducators
    • Maintenance
    • Nutrition Services
    • Custodial
    • Payroll/Accounts Payable
    • CREST Coordinators, Transition Specialists
    • Information Technology Specialists

3.3.2 The Negotiations Team shall:

  • (3.3.2.1) Be chaired by the President.
  • (3.3.2.2) Have no more than 7 members.
  • (3.3.2.3) Contain the rest of the Executive Committee members whenever possible – they shall be offered the right of first refusal for the first four positions.
  • (3.3.2.4) Be selected by the Executive Committee from the members of the Negotiations Committee to represent the classified employees in negotiating with District representatives.
  • (3.3.2.5) Attend negotiations meetings with the district representatives.
  • (3.3.2.6) Accept the contract on a tentative basis and present the 9 proposed contract to the membership for approval. (Once 10 the members have voted to accept the contract as negotiated 11 the School Board will vote to formally ratify it.)

ARTICLE IV - Election Procedures

4.1

Nominations for officers and conference delegates shall be made from the floor at the scheduled March meeting. The Executive Committee shall have the next seven days to solicit written acceptance from the nominees and shall notify the membership of the pending election no less than fifteen days in advance.

4.1.1 Officers shall be determined by those candidates for office that receive a majority of votes cast in the election for a given office. In the event no one candidate does receive a majority of votes, such candidates shall have a runoff election between the two receiving the most votes cast for that office.

4.1.2 Conference delegates shall be determined by those candidates who receive the most votes cast for available delegate positions.

4.1.3 Elections shall be completed in the month of April and the results sent to the membership.

4.1.4 Officers shall be installed at the May meeting and shall assume office at that time.

4.2

The election of officers and delegates shall be by secret ballot by whatever method the Elections Committee chooses in accordance with OSEA’s voting guidelines and procedures.

4.3

The voting window shall be determined by the elections committee.

ARTICLE V - Meetings

5.1

The Executive Committee is responsible for scheduling all Chapter meetings. Specific dates and locations shall be provided at the first meeting of the year.

5.2

They shall schedule no fewer than five regular Chapter meetings. One shall be held before the first day of school and subsequent meetings shall be scheduled in the months of October, January, March, and May.

5.3

All additional Chapter meetings shall be referred to as Special meetings. The business conducted at a Special Meeting shall be limited to the agenda provided.

5.4

In order for business to be conducted a quorum must be met, which constitutes three of the four executive officers and enough active members to bring the total to at least eight.

5.5

The Executive Committee shall inform the members of the details of a Special Meeting no less than seven days in advance, except where stated otherwise. Notice of the meeting shall include the date, time, location, and format.

5.6

The following shall be available to members at each meeting:

  • The Constitution
  • The Bylaws
  • Annual Budget
  • Robert’s Rules of Order and/or Basic Parliamentary Procedures

ARTICLE VI - Conference

6.1

Conference delegates, except those who have been designated as automatic delegates, shall be elected from among the members of this chapter. The Executive Committee shall determine the actual number of delegate positions to be filled, not to exceed the maximum allowable under the OSEA State Constitution, based on available funding and resources.

6.2

The Executive Officers are automatic delegates.

6.3

A Chapter member who is also a member of a State Committee will be an automatic nominee.

6.4

In order to be eligible for nomination, a member shall have attended one Chapter meeting within the current school year.

6.5

Nominees not elected to be delegates and who have received votes will be considered alternates. Alternates will be called upon to fill vacancies according to the number of votes received.

ARTICLE VII - Practices/Procedures

7.1 Records

7.1.1 The chapter secretary shall complete meeting minutes in detail and distribute them to chapter members within fourteen days.

7.1.2 All chapter financial records, meeting minutes and other documents shall be maintained for no less than seven years.

7.1.3 The chapter shall provide a computer each for the secretary and the treasurer to use in performing their duties and for records retention.

7.2 Finances

7.2.1 The chapter shall not provide debit cards.

7.2.2 The chapter will be the custodian of all accounts that are pulling funds from the chapter bank account. There will not be any personal accounts tied to the chapter account.

7.2.3 Expenditures

  • (7.2.3.1) All expenditures under $500 not already approved in the budget must be approved by 3⁄4 of the Executive Committee prior to payment.
  • (7.2.3.2) Expenditures of $500 or over not already approved in the budget must be approved by 2⁄3 of those present at a chapter meeting.
  • (7.2.3.3) All expenditures will have appropriate documentation itemizing the purchase.
  • (7.2.3.4) All income and expenditures will be recorded on the check ledger at the time the money is deposited or spent.
  • (7.2.3.5) The chapter president and treasurer will reconcile the monthly bank statement by using an Excel Spreadsheet or other accounting software using chapter-provided equipment.
  • (7.2.3.6) The chapter treasurer shall report at each meeting all income and expenditures for the time period between chapter meetings.

7.2.4 Reimbursements

  • (7.2.4.1) All reimbursements must be authorized by the Executive Committee and submitted with the appropriate forms and documentation.
  • (7.2.4.2) The chapter will reimburse members at the IRS mileage rate when a reimbursement request form is submitted that includes the locations traveled and how it relates to chapter business. The chapter will not reimburse for the purchase of fuel.
  • (7.2.4.3) Reimbursable purchases which could result in personal benefit, rebates or rewards shall require prior approval from the Executive Committee.
  • (7.2.4.4) The Chapter shall not reimburse members for alcohol.
  • (7.2.4.5) Members who attend conference on State Committee business shall be reimbursed with State funds.

ARTICLE VIII - Amendments and Authority

8.1

All motions to amend the bylaws shall be made by members and shall be provided in writing to the Executive Committee. The Executive Committee shall notify the membership no less than fifteen days prior to a meeting where the amendment shall be presented.

8.2

Amendments shall be adopted by a 2⁄3 vote of the members at a meeting.

8.3

Amendments proposed in order to bring the bylaws into compliance with the OSEA Constitution as a result of resolutions passed at the Annual Conference shall by adopted by a majority vote of the members at a meeting.

8.4

The authority of procedure shall be Robert’s Rules of Order, Revised, where specific conditions are not provided within these Bylaws.
