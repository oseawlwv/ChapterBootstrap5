New Constitution Ratified

(3/12/26) After month's of work by the Constitution Committee, an updated constitution was finally presented to the membership at two seperate readings via virtual Teams meetings. Union members subsequently voted overwhelmingly in favor of ratifying the updates (which may be viewed here), which combines our old constitution and bylaws into a single document (with some appendixes)

The new governing document is based upon an OSEA template, and establishes a mechanism for e-voting, allowing us to once again vote online. Elections will now be executed through the state office, using Election Buddy, which OSEA will pay for. It also includes language to allow unopposed candidates/initiatives to be affirmed automatically without need for a ballot.

The Committee completed it's work last Spring, but due to a backlog of work at the state office, the document was not finalized until recently. The Committee's work was based on a review of the old constitution and bylaws, and a comparison with the OSEA template, which is used by many other chapters. The Committee also solicited feedback from members throughout the process, and made adjustments accordingly.

While the state office has yet to sign off on the final version, the Chapter Executive Board is committed to honoring the clear choice of the members, and has no inclination to revisit until such time as the Chapter membership decides it needs further revision.

Please Report All Incidents!

If you are injured/hurt/assaulted on the job, please remember to always complete an incident report form (en español). Employee Injury reports go to your supervisor or administrator and are sent into HR for files that include all employee injuries for the year. They are sometimes viewed with names redacted and discussed by the Safety Committee to address systemic issues, ensure repairs are made and add new protocols or procedures. The Employee Injury Report should be filled out as promptly as possible, as it is the official notification of injury for an employee should they need medical attention. If medical attention is needed, the employee works with HR to complete the workers compensation paperwork so that workers comp covers the medical costs for the injury.

If your injury is the result of a student action, the first person to address the student-level concern is your principal. Student names should not be put on the Employee Injury Report for FERPA reasons as it becomes a part of your personal medical record. Simply filling out an Employee Injury Report does not lead to adding supports or structures to address the student behavior. You should fill out a referral form at your school to ensure that information is tracked and the team that supports the student is made aware of the situation.

If the student involved is served by an IEP, the added part of the first step would be consultation with the Learning Specialist, who may also bring in the Student Services IC for support. If there are repeated incidents, they will bring in one of the District’s Regulation Skills Coordinators. As a staff member working with this student, it is important that your experiences with the student are a large part of creating any support plans for the student. If the student is on a 504, then consult with their counselor.

Immigration Enforcement: Know Your Rights!

As the federal government continues to ramp up immigration enforcement across the country, it's important to know your rights and responsibilities should agents arrive at your workplace. Oregon is a sanctuary state, which means that staff of public bodies – including public schools – are prohibited from assisting with immigration enforcement, unless officers present a judicial warrant.

But what does that mean for us? OSEA has a number of resources available on our website from organizations that specialize in interactions with immigration authorities. You can find those at the button below. Our national affiliate, the AFT, has also been monitoring the situation and producing materials on how to protect students, keep yourself safe and follow the law when interacting with federal immigration authorities.

OSEA and AFT have compiled resources for immigrants, refugees and supporters. Yo can find them on their website.
