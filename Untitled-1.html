Immigration Enforcement: Know Your Rights!

As the federal government continues to ramp up immigration enforcement across the country, it's important to know your rights and responsibilities should agents arrive at your workplace. Oregon is a sanctuary state, which means that staff of public bodies, including public schools, are prohibited from assisting with immigration enforcement, unless officers present a judicial warrant.

But what does that mean for us? OSEA has a number of resources available on our website from organizations that specialize in interactions with immigration authorities. You can find those at the button below. Our national affiliate, the AFT, has also been monitoring the situation and producing materials on how to protect students, keep yourself safe and follow the law when interacting with federal immigration authorities.

OSEA and AFT have compiled resources for immigrants, refugees and supporters. You can find them on their website.

Certified Furlough Days

Certified Staff voted last spring to take two furlough days during the 2025-2026 school year. The first day was November 10, 2025, and the next one will be May 22, 2026. Our classified union members voted to not lose any working days this school year.

Twelve month classified staff were unaffected by the furlough days, while 10 and 11 month staff are working on both furlough days.

Paraeducators have already made up those two days in separate trainings, and will not be working on the next furlough day, May 22.

On April 17, 2026, Governor Tina Kotek signed an executive order prohibiting districts from redcucing instructional time. She notably did not provide additional money to schools for providing instruction. The new unfunded mandate takes effect in Fall of 2027, so will not affct our current schedule. The EO is vague on what constututes "instructional time", and the state doesn't currentlyknow how many districts have actually instituted furlough days.

Got Membership?

If you have not filled out a membership form and are not paying dues out of your paycheck, you haven’t joined the union yet! With bargaining coming up next year, now is a great time to make sure you are a member! As we prepare to negotiate our next contract, we will be reaching out to members to solidify which areas are most important to them, and we would love to include your voice. Click here to access our membership form. If you have any questions about joining, feel free to email secretary@osea-wlwv.com or shawna@osea.org.

All OSEA members pay the same dues: 1.8 percent of our salary. (In some chapters, members pay a small additional amount for local activities.) It’s a small investment in a bigger cause – dues support a variety of resources that help us represent, protect and empower education employees. Dues allow us to hire professional representatives and legal counsel to level the playing field with our employers. Membership is worth it: union-represented workers make 15% more, on average, than nonunion workers in similar jobs.

As an OSEA member, you have a say in the dues you pay. At the state and chapter level, dues rates are set by our union constitution. They can be updated through a resolution voted on at conference. If you are not an OSEA member and would like to join, please sign up at the OSEA website.
